The interface

Twenty-four knobs. Six rows. One screen.

A full-screen landscape touch interface with interactive knobs, an engine selector, RANDOM, and instant preset save, load, and rename. Built for playing live and sketching fast.

Thirty-nine engines

One braided instrument, thirty-nine voices.

TRESSE braids Mutable Instruments Plaits with a suite of engines from the Tresse hardware. The same engine that voices a lead can become a drum, a resonator, or a wall of grains.

Classic & analog

Subtractive foundations

EastCoast subtractive, dual detuned oscillators, a seven-saw supersaw, waveshaping, and Casio-style phase distortion.

FM & digital

Operators and tables

Six-operator DX-style FM across three banks, two-operator FM with feedback, wavetable scanning, additive harmonics, and NES-style chiptune.

Physical & resonant

Strings and resonators

Karplus-Strong plucked strings, modal and sympathetic-string resonators, comb resonators, and plate and bar models.

Texture & voice

Grains, speech, and air

Granular saw clouds, particle noise, vowel formants and speech, Hammond-style drawbars, and bytebeat.

Percussion

Analog-modelled drums

Analog-modelled bass drum, snare, and hi-hat, plus a noise-based drum engine with pitch envelope.

Complex

Wave-terrain and beyond

Wave-terrain synthesis, a complex oscillator pairing FM with phase wavefold, and a multi-waveform FM engine with inharmonic ratios.

One voice, every engine

The same expressive voice behind all of it.

Whichever engine you pick, it plays through one signal path: a self-stable filter, two envelopes, a routable LFO, and per-voice movement.

// Filter

Self-stable SEM-style filter

Continuous morph from lowpass to bandpass to highpass to notch, with a saturating nonlinearity in the feedback path. Stable at full resonance, no blow-ups.

// Envelopes

Two ADSR envelopes

Independent amplitude and filter envelopes, per voice. The filter envelope drives cutoff by the amount you set.

// LFO

Six shapes, routable

Sine, triangle, saw, square, sample-and-hold, and random, sent to pitch, timbre, morph, harmonics, filter, or amplitude.

// Voice

Detune, spread, portamento

Per-voice pitch detune, stereo spread across the field, and portamento glide from the previous note, all working in eight-voice polyphony.

// Wavefolder

Post-filter wavefolder

A final wavefolder for extra bite, sitting before a transparent soft-clip ceiling that keeps the output in check.
